Projects Launching Soon and Other Ongoing Interests

Browser Extension: I'm currently developing a browser extension that hides YouTube videos behind a practice problem set, helping users focus on learning a topic of their choice even while they browse the internet. The extension will prompt users to solve a few problems of increasing difficulty related to their chosen subject before granting access to the video content. I have a working prototype that uses Ollama to create and grade the problems so that I'm not forces to answer questions that I wrote myself. Once complete, I plan to release the extension on the Chrome Web Store and make it open source on GitHub.

Photo Location Tool: I'm also developing a tool that aims to guess the location of a photograph, or a arc along which a photograph could have been taken, based on a wireframe representation of the mountains and terrain visible in the image. The tool uses computer vision techniques to identify key features in the photo, then matches those features against a database of geotagged elevation data to narrow down possible locations. The goal is to help outdoor enthusiasts pinpoint where photos were taken, or to help search and rescue teams locate lost individuals. This project was motivated by my own experiences of trying to identify locations in my outdoor photos using the CalTopo wireframe overlay, and wanting to automate that process.

Other Interests: I'm also exploring various other projects and ideas in geometric deep learning, applied measure theory, and computer vision. If you have any suggestions or would like to collaborate, feel free to reach out!
